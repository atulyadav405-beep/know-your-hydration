The core issue

Water is not hydration. It's just wet.

Hydration is your body holding onto fluid. That requires electrolytes — specifically sodium — to trigger the mechanisms that actually retain water in your blood and cells.

Plain water
58%
retained by the body. The rest is urinated out within 2 hours — with plasma sodium declining at −2.48 mmol/L/hr.
Maughan AJCN 2016 ↗
vs
Electrolyte drink
74–77%
retained. Sodium triggers ADH release and SGLT1 cotransport — your body's two primary water-retention mechanisms working together.

What this means for your session
A 90-minute run at moderate intensity can deplete 900–1400mg of sodium through sweat. If you replace that with plain water, your plasma sodium drops, your kidneys flush the water back out, and you end the session worse than when you started — even if you "drank enough."
